# Scenario Response Guide

## Purpose

This guide provides recommended responses for common customer scenarios. Each scenario includes trigger phrases, required actions, and suggested scripts.

---

## Scenario 1: Customer Lost Their Job

### Trigger Phrases
- "I lost my job"
- "I was laid off"
- "I'm unemployed now"
- "The company closed down"
- "My hours were cut"

### Required Actions
1. Express empathy
2. Offer hardship program
3. Explore payment options
4. Document situation

### Recommended Response

**Step 1: Acknowledge**
> "I'm really sorry to hear that. Losing your job is incredibly stressful, especially when you have bills to manage."

**Step 2: Offer Hardship**
> "We have a hardship program that's specifically designed for situations like yours. It could include reduced payments or even a temporary pause while you get back on your feet. Would you like me to explain your options?"

**Step 3: Explore Options**
> "What kind of payment would be manageable for you right now? Even a smaller amount can help keep the account in good standing while you look for work."

**Step 4: Set Review**
> "Let's set up a review in three months to see how things are going. If your situation changes, please call us and we can adjust."

### Do NOT Say
- "Everyone has problems"
- "Can you borrow from someone?"
- "You still need to pay something"

## Scenario 2: Customer Has Medical Issues

### Trigger Phrases
- "I'm sick"
- "I was in hospital"
- "I have medical bills"
- "I'm dealing with an illness"
- "I had surgery"

### Required Actions
1. Express genuine concern
2. Don't pressure for details
3. Offer hardship program
4. Allow extra time

### Recommended Response

**Step 1: Acknowledge**
> "I'm sorry to hear you're dealing with health issues. That must be really difficult on top of everything else."

**Step 2: Offer Support**
> "Your health comes first. We have a hardship program that can help during this time. We can look at reducing payments or pausing them while you focus on recovery."

**Step 3: Don't Pry**
> "You don't need to tell me the details - I just want to make sure we find something that works for you."

### Do NOT Say
- "What exactly is wrong?"
- "How long will you be sick?"
- "Can you still pay something?"

---

## Scenario 3: Customer Is Angry About Fees

### Trigger Phrases
- "These fees are outrageous"
- "I shouldn't have to pay this"
- "This is unfair"
- "I want these fees removed"

### Required Actions
1. Let them express frustration
2. Acknowledge their concern
3. Explain the fees
4. Explore options (waiver if applicable)

### Recommended Response

**Step 1: Listen and Acknowledge**
> "I understand the fees are frustrating, and I appreciate you raising this with me."

**Step 2: Explain**
> "These fees are applied when payments are missed. I know that doesn't make them easier to deal with."

**Step 3: Offer Options**
> "Let me see what I can do. If we can set up a regular payment arrangement, I may be able to waive some of these fees as a goodwill gesture."

### Do NOT Say
- "That's just policy"
- "There's nothing I can do"
- "You should have paid on time"

---

## Scenario 4: Customer Disputes the Amount

### Trigger Phrases
- "That's not right"
- "I don't owe that much"
- "There's a mistake"
- "I already paid this"

### Required Actions
1. Take the concern seriously
2. Explain dispute process
3. Note account as disputed
4. Provide timeline

### Recommended Response

**Step 1: Acknowledge**
> "I understand you believe there's an error. Let me look into this with you."

**Step 2: Gather Information**
> "Can you tell me what amount you believe is correct, and do you have any documentation, like receipts or bank statements?"

**Step 3: Explain Process**
> "I'm going to mark this account as disputed. Our team will investigate, which usually takes 10-14 business days. While that's happening, we won't take any further action on the account."

**Step 4: Confirm**
> "You have the right to dispute this debt. I'll send you written confirmation of your dispute today."

## Scenario 5: Customer Can't Pay Anything

### Trigger Phrases
- "I have nothing"
- "I can't pay anything"
- "I'm completely broke"
- "Every cent is accounted for"

### Required Actions
1. Show empathy
2. Explore hardship options
3. Consider temporary pause
4. Set review date

### Recommended Response

**Step 1: Acknowledge**
> "I hear you. It sounds like things are really tight right now."

**Step 2: Explore**
> "Can I ask what's making it difficult? Understanding your situation helps me find the right options for you."

**Step 3: Offer Solutions**
> "We can look at a temporary pause on payments - that gives you some breathing room without the account getting worse. Would that help?"

**Step 4: Set Expectations**
> "This would be for [timeframe], and then we'd review to see how things are going. Does that sound reasonable?"

## Scenario 6: Customer Threatens to Go to AFCA

### Trigger Phrases
- "I'm going to AFCA"
- "I'll report you to the ombudsman"
- "I'm making a formal complaint"

### Required Actions
1. Don't become defensive
2. Acknowledge their right
3. Try to resolve first
4. Provide complaint information

### Recommended Response

**Step 1: Acknowledge**
> "You absolutely have the right to contact AFCA if you're not satisfied. Before you do, can I try to resolve this for you today?"

**Step 2: Understand**
> "What would need to happen for you to feel this is resolved?"

**Step 3: Provide Information (if proceeding)**
> "If you'd like to proceed with a complaint, you can reach AFCA at 1800 931 678 or afca.org.au. Would you like me to also escalate this internally?"

---

## Scenario 7: Customer Mentions Divorce/Separation

### Trigger Phrases
- "We're getting divorced"
- "My partner left"
- "I'm separating from my spouse"
- "It's a joint account but we've split"

### Required Actions
1. Express sympathy
2. Offer hardship program
3. Explain account obligations
4. Suggest financial counseling

### Recommended Response

**Step 1: Acknowledge**
> "I'm sorry to hear you're going through that. Separations are stressful enough without financial pressure on top."

**Step 2: Address Practicalities**
> "I need to let you know that if this is a joint account, both parties remain responsible. However, we have options that can help during this transition."

**Step 3: Offer Support**
> "Would you like information about our hardship program? We also have information about free financial counseling services that can help you work through your options."
