# Empathy Phrases Guide

## Purpose

This guide provides approved empathy phrases for various situations. Using appropriate empathy language is a key factor in coaching scores and customer satisfaction.

## The Empathy Formula

**Acknowledge + Validate + Support**

1. **Acknowledge**: Recognize their situation or feeling
2. **Validate**: Show their reaction is understandable
3. **Support**: Offer to help

**Example**:
> "I can hear how frustrating this is [Acknowledge]. Anyone would feel the same way in your situation [Validate]. Let me see what I can do to help [Support]."

---

## Phrases for Acknowledging Feelings

### Frustration
- "I can hear how frustrated you are."
- "I understand this is really frustrating."
- "That does sound incredibly frustrating."

### Stress/Overwhelm
- "That sounds really stressful."
- "It sounds like you're dealing with a lot right now."
- "I can only imagine how overwhelming this must be."

### Anger
- "I can hear how upset you are."
- "I understand you're angry about this."
- "Your frustration is completely understandable."

### Sadness
- "I'm sorry you're going through this."
- "That must be really hard."
- "This sounds like a difficult time for you."

### Confusion
- "I can see how this would be confusing."
- "Let me help clarify that for you."
- "I understand this is complicated."

---

## Phrases for Validating Experiences

### General Validation
- "I would feel the same way."
- "That's completely understandable."
- "You have every right to feel that way."
- "Anyone in your situation would feel the same."

### Specific Situations
- "Losing your job is incredibly stressful - I can't imagine dealing with bills on top of that."
- "Medical issues are hard enough without financial worries."
- "Going through a separation is difficult, and money pressures make it even harder."

---

## Phrases for Offering Support

### Commitment to Help
- "I want to help you sort this out."
- "Let me see what I can do for you."
- "I'm going to do my best to find a solution."
- "Let's work together on this."

### Taking Ownership
- "I'll personally make sure this is handled."
- "I'm going to take care of this for you."
- "Leave this with me."

### Partnership Language
- "We'll figure this out together."
- "Let's see what options we have."
- "We can work through this."

---

## Phrases to Avoid (Anti-Empathy)

### Dismissive Language
| DON'T Say | DO Say Instead |
|-----------|----------------|
| "I understand, BUT..." | "I understand, AND here's what we can do..." |
| "Everyone has problems" | "Your situation is important" |
| "That's not my fault" | "Let me see how I can help" |
| "You should have..." | "Let's focus on solutions" |
| "There's nothing I can do" | "Here's what I can do" |
| "Calm down" | "I hear your frustration" |
| "I don't know why you're upset" | "I understand why you're upset" |

### Blaming Language
| DON'T Say | DO Say Instead |
|-----------|----------------|
| "You got yourself into this" | "Let's work on moving forward" |
| "That's your responsibility" | "I can help you with this" |
| "You should have paid" | "Let's find a solution now" |
| "That's your problem" | "Let me see how I can help" |

### Minimizing Language
| DON'T Say | DO Say Instead |
|-----------|----------------|
| "It's not that bad" | "I understand this is difficult" |
| "Just pay what you can" | "Let's find what works for you" |
| "Others have it worse" | "Your situation matters" |
| "At least..." | "I understand..." |

## Empathy by Scenario

### Financial Hardship
> "I'm really sorry to hear you're going through this. Financial stress is incredibly difficult, and it takes courage to reach out. I want to help find a solution that works for you."

### Job Loss
> "Losing your job is one of the most stressful things anyone can go through. I'm sorry you're dealing with this. Let's see what we can do to take some pressure off while you find new work."

### Medical Issues
> "I'm sorry to hear about your health challenges. Your wellbeing is the most important thing right now. We have options to help take financial stress off your plate while you focus on recovery."

### Family Issues (Divorce, Death)
> "I'm very sorry for what you're going through. Major life changes are hard enough without worrying about money. Let me explain some options that might help during this time."

### Angry Customer
> "I hear your frustration, and I'm sorry your experience with us hasn't been what it should be. I want to do everything I can to make this right for you today."

## Building Empathy Into the Call

### Opening Empathy
After customer explains situation:
> "Thank you for sharing that with me. I can imagine this hasn't been easy."

### Mid-Call Empathy
When customer shows emotion:
> "I can hear this is really affecting you. Let's pause for a moment - how are you doing?"

### Closing Empathy
Before ending call:
> "I hope things get better for you soon. Please don't hesitate to call us if your situation changes."

## Empathy Scoring Rubric

### Score 9-10 (Exceptional)
- Multiple empathy statements throughout
- Validates feelings naturally
- Customer feels genuinely heard
- Empathy matched to specific situation

### Score 7-8 (Good)
- Some empathy statements
- Basic acknowledgment of feelings
- Shows understanding

### Score 5-6 (Adequate)
- Minimal empathy
- Polite but not warm
- Misses emotional cues

### Score 3-4 (Poor)
- Little to no empathy
- Dismissive responses
- Rushes past customer concerns

### Score 1-2 (Critical)
- Hostile or blaming
- Completely ignores distress
- Makes customer feel worse
